# Glass — Product Brief

## Purpose

Turn refraction and chromatic aberration into an object the user can hold, move, and understand through touch.

## Core model

A digital lens sits above an image. Its position and optical parameters change the image locally. The visitor experiences a simple physical relationship: move the glass, and reality bends beneath it.

## Core loop

Move the lens, observe the distortion, compare it with the undistorted image, and develop an intuition for the effect.

## Novelty

The experiment gives a normally invisible visual computation a location, boundary, and physical response.

## Discovery and proof

The visitor should feel: “This digital surface has physical behavior.” The concept works when the optical effect is unmistakable within the first interaction without requiring the technical explanation.

## Non-goals

This is not a filter-settings panel or an optics textbook. Controls exist only to make the physical metaphor more legible.
