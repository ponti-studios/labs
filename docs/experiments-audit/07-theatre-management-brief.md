# Theatre Management — Product Brief

## Purpose

Show a theatre as a living system where programming choices carry both cultural and economic consequences.

## Core model

The user allocates screens, chooses a lineup, and adjusts market and pricing conditions. The model translates those decisions into attendance, revenue, expenses, utilization, and profit.

## Core loop

Choose a lineup, change one strategic assumption, observe the financial and attendance consequences, and reconsider the tradeoff.

## Novelty

It treats a theatre as a cultural institution under economic constraint rather than a neutral spreadsheet or a purely commercial venue.

## Discovery and proof

The visitor should feel: “Every programming decision is also a decision about survival.” The concept works when one decision produces a visible downstream consequence without requiring an explanation of the calculation code.

## Non-goals

This is not a generic financial dashboard or a cinema operations suite. The model should stay focused on the tension between what a theatre wants to show and what keeps it alive.
